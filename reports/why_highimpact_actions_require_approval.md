# Why High-Impact Actions Require Approval

## Evidence-Grounded Findings

### Core Principle
The knowledge base consistently establishes that **high-impact agents require explicit human approval gates before executing persistent actions**. This is the foundational rule governing high-impact agent operations.

### Key Rationale

1. **Risk Mitigation Through Human Oversight**
   High-impact actions carry consequences that are lasting, irreversible, or operationally significant. Requiring approval ensures that a human remains in the decision loop before such actions are taken, preventing unintended or unauthorized outcomes.

2. **Persistent Actions Demand a Gate**
   The distinction between transient actions (e.g., reading data, generating a one-off response) and persistent actions (e.g., writing files, modifying records, sending communications, executing system changes) is critical. Persistent actions have ongoing effects. The approval gate acts as a safety interlock: no persistent action proceeds without explicit human sign-off.

3. **Accountability and Control**
   Approval gates preserve human agency and accountability. The human operator retains the final say over consequential operations, which is essential for trust, compliance, and governance in autonomous or semi-autonomous agent systems.

4. **Prevention of Unauthorized Escalation**
   Without an approval gate, a high-impact agent could autonomously execute a chain of persistent actions that compound in effect. The gate breaks that chain, forcing a checkpoint where the human can evaluate, modify, or halt the action.
